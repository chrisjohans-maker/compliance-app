Help & regulatory rules

This tool uses AI to pre-screen marketing content against FINRA Rule 2210 and NFA Rule 2-29 before it reaches the compliance team. It flags language that may require revision and explains why — saving review cycles for both sides.

How to submit
1
Paste your content
Copy your draft — email, web copy, social post, video script, or ad — and paste it into the text field. For a URL, use the URL tab and also paste the page text for best results.
2
Set context fields
Select the correct asset class, communication type, and audience. These change which rules the AI applies — retail social ads are reviewed more strictly than institutional pieces, and options content triggers additional requirements.
3
Run the analysis
Click Analyze. The tool returns a compliance score (0–100), flagged items with the specific rule violated, and a suggested rewrite for each issue. Takes 5–15 seconds.
4
Review flags and send
Expand each flag to read the issue and suggested fix. Address high-severity items before submitting. Then click Add to queue — compliance will pick it up and issue a final disposition.
Common reasons content gets flagged
HIGH
Performance claims without balance
Saying a strategy "outperforms" or has "higher returns" without equal-prominence risk disclosure. Fix: add a risk caveat in the same sentence or immediately adjacent.
HIGH
Predictive language
Phrases like "will increase," "is likely to rise," or "signals an upward move." Fix: use "may," "historically," or "some traders believe."
MED
Superlatives and unsupported comparisons
"Highly effective," "best approach," or "superior risk/reward" without supporting data. Fix: soften to "one approach" or "can be effective."
MED
Missing or buried risk disclosure
Risk language only in fine print doesn't satisfy FINRA's "clear and prominent" standard. Fix: add a risk callout near the top of the piece.
LOW
Promotional framing in educational content
Phrases like "opportunities others don't see" blur the line between education and promotion. Fix: use neutral, descriptive language.
Important
A passing AI score is not final approval. All content still requires a compliance reviewer's disposition before publication. The AI pre-screen reduces back-and-forth but does not replace the Principal review required under FINRA and NFA rules.

The tool sends submitted content to Claude (Anthropic) via API along with your firm's precedent library and the applicable regulatory framework. Every compliance decision you make is fed back into the precedent library and used to calibrate future analyses.

Review workflow
1
Pick up items from the queue
The Review Queue tab shows all pending submissions with their AI score and flag count. Higher flag counts and lower scores warrant closer scrutiny.
2
Review the AI analysis
Each flagged phrase is mapped to a specific rule with a suggested fix. The Precedent Matches section shows if any language has been reviewed before — and how it was previously treated.
3
Make per-flag decisions
Expand each flag and choose: Keep flagged (confirms the finding, adds to flagged patterns) or Mark acceptable (overrides the AI, saves as approved pattern). These decisions train the model.
4
Issue a disposition
Select Pass, Conditional Pass, or Fail. Add a reviewer note to document your rationale — this becomes part of the audit trail.
5
The model learns
Every flag decision updates the Precedent Library. Future submissions are scored against this growing library — reducing false positives over time.
Disposition standards
PASS
Content is compliant as submitted
No material issues under FINRA 2210 or NFA 2-29. Content may be published without revision.
COND
Compliant with specified revisions
One or more issues must be addressed before publication. Use the reviewer note to specify required changes. Marketing must resubmit for final approval after revisions.
FAIL
Material compliance issues present
Content contains violations that cannot be addressed with minor edits — e.g., prohibited performance guarantees, missing required disclosures, or content not suitable for the stated audience. Full rewrite required.
Recordkeeping note
Use Export JSON in Settings to archive all decisions periodically. This supplements your firm's required books and records under FINRA Rule 4511 and NFA Compliance Rule 2-10. This tool does not substitute for your firm's existing principal approval recordkeeping system.

This tool applies the following regulatory frameworks. The AI maps every flag to a specific rule citation.

FINRA Rule 2210 — Communications with the public
Applies to: securities, options, ETFs · Retail and institutional
2210(d)(1)
General standards
Content must be fair, balanced, and not contain any false, exaggerated, unwarranted, promissory, or misleading statement. Flags: superlatives without substantiation, promissory language, claims implying certainty about future performance.
2210(d)(1)(B)
Risk disclosure — prominence
When a communication discusses potential rewards, it must give fair and balanced treatment to associated risks with equal prominence. Risk buried in footnotes alone does not satisfy this requirement for retail communications.
2210(d)(2)
Performance data
Past performance must not imply future results. Required time periods apply (1-year, 5-year, 10-year, since inception). Flags any performance figure not accompanied by the standard disclaimer.
2210(d)(7)
Comparisons
Comparisons between products or strategies must be fair and all material differences must be disclosed. Flags comparative claims that lack substantiation or omit material differences.
2210(c)
Principal review
Retail communications must be reviewed and approved by a registered principal prior to use. This tool's AI analysis supports but does not satisfy that requirement.
FINRA Rule 2220 — Options communications
Applies to: options · Retail
2220(b)
ODD delivery obligation
Any retail communication promoting options trading must reference the Characteristics and Risks of Standardized Options (ODD) and provide or link to it.
2220(c)
Suitability language
Options communications must state that options are not suitable for all investors and may expose investors to potentially significant losses.
NFA Compliance Rule 2-29 — Communications with the public
Applies to: futures, commodities, forex · Retail and institutional
2-29(b)
Fair and balanced
Same core standard as FINRA 2210(d)(1). Applied to all futures and forex content regardless of whether FINRA also applies.
2-29(b)(1)
Hypothetical performance disclaimer
Any hypothetical or simulated performance result must include the NFA's mandated verbatim disclaimer. The tool flags any hypothetical figure missing this language.
2-29(b)(2)
Prominence of risk disclosure
Risk disclosure must appear on the same page as, and in close proximity to, any promotional claims. Footer-only disclosure is insufficient under NFA review.
2-29(c)
Testimonials
Testimonials must disclose whether the speaker is a customer and whether they were compensated. Flags any quote or endorsement missing these disclosures.
Crypto — applicable frameworks
Applies to: digital assets · Regulatory status: evolving
FINRA 2210
If offered through a FINRA broker-dealer
Crypto communications are treated as retail communications under Rule 2210. FINRA Regulatory Notice 22-08 emphasizes that promotional content must not overstate potential gains or understate risks including total loss of principal.
SEC / Howey
"Earn," yield, and staking language
Language promoting crypto yield, staking rewards, or "earn" programs may imply a security under the Howey test. Flagged for compliance review given ongoing regulatory uncertainty.
General
Total loss disclosure
Crypto communications must disclose that the value of any cryptocurrency may go to zero, including stablecoins pegged to fiat currency or commodities.
Influencer & third-party content
Applies to: all asset classes · Retail
FINRA 2210
Adopted communications
If the firm endorses, shares, reposts, or links to third-party content, that content becomes an "adopted communication" and must comply with the same standards as firm-produced content. Compensation to influencers must be disclosed.
SEC 206(4)-1
Testimonials and endorsements
Requires disclosure of material conflicts, compensation, and whether the endorser is a client. The spirit of this rule is applied by FINRA examiners to broker-dealer testimonial content.
