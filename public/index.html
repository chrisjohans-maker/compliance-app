Help & regulatory rules

This tool pre-screens your marketing content against FINRA Rule 2210 and NFA Rule 2-29 before it reaches the compliance team. Submit content, review AI flags, and send to Pending Compliance Review. Compliance will review and either approve or send back with revision notes.

How to submit
1
Paste, upload, or enter a URL
Use the New Submission tab to paste copy, drop a PDF, or enter a page URL. Select the asset class, communication type, and audience.
2
Review AI analysis
The tool scores your content and flags issues with specific rule citations. Expand each flag to see a suggested rewrite. Apply rewrites directly in the revised article panel.
3
Send to Pending Compliance Review
Click Send to Pending Review. Compliance will pick it up from their queue and either approve it or send it back with revision notes.
4
Act on revision requests
If compliance sends it back, the item appears in Revision Requested with their notes. Make the changes and resubmit — it will return to Pending Compliance Review tagged as a Resubmission.
Important
A passing AI score is not final approval. All content requires a compliance disposition before publication. The AI pre-screen reduces review cycles but does not replace principal approval under FINRA and NFA rules.

Marketing submits content to the Pending Compliance Review queue. Each submission includes an AI pre-screen with rule-mapped flags. Your job is to review, make per-flag decisions, and issue a disposition. Every decision trains the precedent library.

Review workflow
1
Pick up items from Pending Compliance Review
Items tagged "Resubmission" have been through a prior round — check compliance notes from the previous cycle. Items without a tag are first-time submissions.
2
Review AI flags and make per-flag decisions
Expand each flag and choose Keep flagged or Mark acceptable. Every decision updates the precedent library and calibrates future analyses.
3
Issue a disposition
Select Request revision (sends to Revision Requested with your notes) or Approve (moves to Approved). Only compliance can issue approvals.
Recordkeeping
Use Export JSON in Settings to archive decisions periodically. This supplements required records under FINRA Rule 4511 and NFA Rule 2-10. This tool does not replace your firm's existing principal approval recordkeeping system.

The AI maps every flag to a specific rule citation from the frameworks below.

FINRA Rule 2210 — Communications with the public
Securities, options, ETFs · Retail and institutional
2210(d)(1)
General standards
Content must be fair, balanced, and not contain false, exaggerated, unwarranted, promissory, or misleading statements. Flags superlatives, promissory language, and claims implying certainty about future performance.
2210(d)(1)(B)
Risk disclosure prominence
Potential rewards must give equal prominence to associated risks. Risk buried in footnotes alone does not satisfy this for retail communications.
2210(d)(2)
Performance data
Past performance must not imply future results. Required time periods apply. Flags any figure not accompanied by the standard disclaimer.
2210(c)
Principal review
Retail communications must be reviewed and approved by a registered principal. This tool's AI analysis supports but does not satisfy that requirement.
FINRA Rule 2220 — Options communications
Options · Retail
2220(b)
ODD delivery
Retail options communications must reference and link to the Characteristics and Risks of Standardized Options.
2220(c)
Suitability language
Must state options are not suitable for all investors and may expose investors to potentially significant losses.
NFA Compliance Rule 2-29 — Communications with the public
Futures, commodities, forex · Retail and institutional
2-29(b)(1)
Hypothetical performance disclaimer
Any hypothetical or simulated performance must include the NFA's mandated verbatim disclaimer.
2-29(b)(2)
Risk disclosure prominence
Risk disclosure must appear on the same page as promotional claims. Footer-only is insufficient.
2-29(c)
Testimonials
Must disclose whether the speaker is a customer and whether compensated.
Crypto
Digital assets · Regulatory status: evolving
FINRA 2210
If offered through a FINRA BD
Treated as retail communications. FINRA Notice 22-08: must not overstate gains or understate risks including total loss.
General
Total loss disclosure
Must disclose the value of any cryptocurrency may go to zero, including stablecoins.
